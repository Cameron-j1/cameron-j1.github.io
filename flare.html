01 • Concept overview

Objective & motivation

My Robotics Studio 1 group and I set out to design an incredibly robust and scalable robotics concept that could enhance both safety and operational efficiency for frontline agencies such as the Rural Fire Service and NSW National Parks & Wildlife Service.

The result is F.L.A.R.E — Firefighting using Localisation and Advanced Real‑time Environment Mapping — a dual‑robot system concept that provides rich situational awareness, intelligent fire‑localisation, and targeted autonomous response.

System architecture

Our demonstration combines a UAV for autonomous aerial fire scanning and sweeping using RGB and IR camera inputs, with a UGV that autonomously navigates to identified fires and extinguishes them using a LiDAR‑based SLAM and Nav2 stack. Both platforms are orchestrated via a unified ROS 2‑based GUI.

The full workflow — from fire detection to UGV deployment and suppression — is simulated with Gazebo Ignition and developed entirely in ROS 2, enabling repeatable testing and rapid iteration.

  • UAV: Autonomous aerial sweep, RGB + IR inputs, fire localisation & bounding.
  • UGV: LiDAR SLAM, Nav2‑based navigation, autonomous fire approach & extinguishing behaviour.
  • GUI: Central mission control tying together perception, planning, telemetry, and teleoperation.

Scalability & modularity

The architecture is intentionally scalable and modular, allowing multiple UGVs and UAVs to operate concurrently and independently navigate to, and extinguish, separate fires. Configuration parameters and topics are structured so that new platforms can be added with minimal integration overhead.

02 • Human‑centred GUI

Steam Deck‑optimised operator experience

A core focus of the project was a polished, intuitive user interface designed to run seamlessly on a Steam Deck. By prioritising human‑centred design, we ensured the operator maintains critical situational awareness in a compact, mobile form factor.

GUI capabilities

  • Human‑centred controls to easily start and stop autonomous routines.
  • Immediate feedback on fire position and estimated size, battery levels, and mission statistics.
  • Live data streams including thermal imaging, aerial map view, and real‑time RGB camera feeds over ROS 2.
  • Precise positional tracking for both UAV and UGV in the environment.
  • Reliable manual teleoperation override to ensure the operator always remains in control.
